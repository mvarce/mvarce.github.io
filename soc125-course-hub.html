SPSS walkthrough — your first analysis

A sample of the kind of step-by-step guide you'll find for each technique. This one runs descriptive statistics on a single variable.

  1. Open your .sav file in SPSS, then look at the Variable View to confirm each variable's measurement level (nominal, ordinal, or scale).
  2. Go to Analyze → Descriptive Statistics → Frequencies.
  3. Move your variable into the box, click Statistics, and check mean, median, mode, and standard deviation.
  4. Click Charts → Histograms and turn on the normal curve overlay.
  5. Run it, then read the output the way we practice in class: shape first, then center, then spread.
* The same analysis as SPSS syntax — paste into a Syntax window and run.
FREQUENCIES VARIABLES=income
  /STATISTICS=MEAN MEDIAN MODE STDDEV
  /HISTOGRAM NORMAL.

Assignments & project guides

What's being asked of you

Every assignment here is really practice in one thing: using data to make a careful, honest claim about the social world.

Signature project

The research paper

You'll build one complete study from question to conclusion. This is where everything in the course comes together.

  1. Ask a sociological question worth answering — e.g. gender inequality in STEM, or LGBT+ identification and student success.
  2. Run a short literature review and decide which statistical method actually fits your question.
  3. Collect survey data, analyze it in SPSS, and interpret what you find.
  4. Write it up as an eight-page, double-spaced paper that draws an honest conclusion from your evidence.
